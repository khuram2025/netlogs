Appendix
A Appendix — Methodology & Data Sources

Posture score

Overall score starts at 100 and is deducted for material findings: critical permit rules (−12 each), high permit rules (−5 each, capped at 20), unlogged permits (−3 each, capped at 15), average permissiveness (−0.3× the KPI value), implicit-deny flows (−5 once), and soft penalties for shadowed/redundant clutter. Bands: ≥80 Low, ≥60 Moderate, ≥40 High, below 40 Critical.

Permissiveness scoring

Weighted flag sum — any-src-zone (+10), any-dst-zone (+10), any-src-addr (+25), any-dst-addr (+25), any-svc (+20), log-off on a permit (+10). Bands: ≥80 CRITICAL, ≥60 HIGH, ≥40 MEDIUM, otherwise LOW. Deny and disabled rules are excluded on the grounds that a broad deny is a control, not an exposure.

Shadowed / redundant detection

Rule N is shadowed or redundant when every dimension of N is a (non-strict) subset of an earlier rule M. Dimensions checked: source/destination zone, source/destination address, service, and application (PAN-OS App-ID). The App-ID check prevents a broad application:icmp-timestamp deny from wrongly claiming coverage over unrelated rules that share only any match fields.

Data sources

Rule base, address objects and service objects come from the snapshot listed on the cover page (SSH or XML/REST API fetch; for PAN-OS, pre-rulebase + local rulebase + post-rulebase are merged in evaluation order). Hit counts and implicit-deny flows come from the ingested syslog stream over the 30-day window; when logs are not yet joined, those sections are omitted.

Out of scope

  • Per-packet simulation of ingress ACL, NAT, and egress paths — the engine uses first-match against the configured rule base.
  • Application-level signature accuracy (we record the configured App-ID, not the classifier's confidence).
  • Threat prevention profiles (IPS/AV/URL/DNS) — reviewed separately.
  • Panorama push history — only the committed state seen by the managed firewall is analysed.
